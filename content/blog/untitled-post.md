# Best Chin Chin Brand: Top Nigerian Snack Brands Ranked

Chin chin is one of the most beloved snacks across West Africa — a crispy, crunchy, fried dough treat that has found its way from home kitchens to commercial shelves worldwide. But with so many brands now producing chin chin at scale, which ones actually deliver on taste, texture, and quality?

We tested and compared the most popular chin chin brands to help you find the best one for your next snack run.

## What Makes a Great Chin Chin?

Before diving into our rankings, here's what we evaluated each brand on:

- **Crunch factor** — The best chin chin should snap cleanly, not crumble or feel stale.
- **Flavour balance** — Sweetness should complement, not overpower. Some brands add nutmeg, vanilla, or milk for depth.
- **Ingredient quality** — Fewer artificial additives and preservatives score higher.
- **Texture consistency** — Every piece should be evenly fried, with no doughy centres.
- **Value for money** — How much chin chin do you actually get for the price?

## Top Chin Chin Brands Ranked

### 1. Aunty Funmi's Chin Chin

A household favourite for good reason. Aunty Funmi's delivers a perfectly balanced chin chin — crunchy, lightly sweet, and made with simple, recognisable ingredients. The pieces are uniform in size and consistently well-fried.

**Best for:** Traditionalists who want chin chin that tastes homemade.

### 2. Okin Chin Chin

Okin has been in the Nigerian snack game for decades. Their chin chin is harder and crunchier than most, which fans love. It holds up well in storage and travels without crumbling.

**Best for:** Those who prefer an extra-crunchy bite.

### 3. Obalende Chin Chin

A popular choice in Lagos and beyond, Obalende chin chin offers a slightly buttery flavour with a lighter texture. It's a crowd-pleaser at parties and events.

**Best for:** Sharing at gatherings or pairing with a cold drink.

### 4. Nkulenu's Chin Chin

Coming from Ghana, Nkulenu's brings a slightly different take on chin chin — a touch less sweet with a firmer bite. A solid choice if you enjoy West African snacks beyond Nigerian brands.

**Best for:** Trying a Ghanaian twist on the classic.

### 5. Small Chops Lagos Chin Chin

A newer brand making waves on social media, Small Chops Lagos focuses on artisanal, small-batch chin chin with creative flavours like coconut and cinnamon.

**Best for:** Adventurous snackers looking for unique flavours.

## How to Store Chin Chin

No matter which brand you choose, proper storage keeps your chin chin fresh:

- Store in an **airtight container** at room temperature.
- Avoid humid environments — moisture is chin chin's worst enemy.
- Most brands stay fresh for **2–4 weeks** when stored properly.

## Making Your Own vs. Buying a Brand

While nothing beats fresh, homemade chin chin straight from the pot, commercial brands offer convenience and consistency. If you're short on time or want a reliable snack for the road, the brands above are your best bet.

For those who prefer to make their own, the key ingredients are flour, sugar, butter or margarine, eggs, nutmeg, and milk — fried in vegetable oil until golden brown.

## Final Verdict

The **best chin chin brand** ultimately depends on your personal preference. If you value a homemade taste, Aunty Funmi's is hard to beat. For maximum crunch, Okin is the way to go. And if you want something different, Small Chops Lagos brings exciting new flavours to the table.

Whichever brand you choose, chin chin remains one of the greatest snack inventions to come out of West Africa — and it's only getting better.
